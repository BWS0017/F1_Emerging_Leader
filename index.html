HERSEY & BLANCHARD SITUATIONAL LEADERSHIP ILM ASSURED
LEADERSHIP
Influence. Direction. Trust.
The practice of influencing others toward a shared goal — through vision, consistent behaviour, and the trust that comes from demonstrating you care about both results and people.
Leadership asks: "Where are we going — and why does it matter?"
VS
MANAGEMENT
Plan. Organise. Deliver.
The practice of planning, organising, and controlling resources and processes to achieve defined outcomes reliably, efficiently, and on time.
Management asks: "How do we get there — and are we on track?"
THE RACE LINE 🏁
Leadership is relational and directional. Management is structural and operational. Both are essential. Neither is superior. The skill is knowing which one this moment demands.

MANAGEMENT
FOCUS

The critical insight: Emerging leaders often default to management because it feels safer. Leadership requires courage — to focus on people and the longer view even when the task is urgent.

HASSAN'S MOMENT
"So the question isn't what kind of leader I am. It's what kind of leader this person needs right now." — Hassan, Lap 2 close
1
Authority Culture Shapes the Default
In Gulf organisations, directive leadership (S1) is often the culturally expected norm — regardless of team member experience. Leaders who only direct are not seen as flexible. They are seen as the only option.
In practice: Defaulting to S1 with a D4 team member can erode motivation and signal a lack of trust — especially damaging in high-performing individuals.
2
Psychological Safety Enables Readiness
Team members cannot progress from D1 to D4 unless they feel safe to try, fail, and grow. Leaders who only direct create dependency. Leaders who build safety create capability.
In practice: Build safety gradually — start with S2 Coaching (explain the why, invite input) before moving to S3 and S4 with developing team members.
3
Adapting Your Style Is Precision
The most common leadership mistake is applying the same style to every team member. Situational leadership is not inconsistency — it is precision. Knowing when to step in and when to step back is the mark of an expert leader.
"Adapting your style is not weakness. It is the highest expression of leadership intelligence." — Dr Beverley WilsonSmith
